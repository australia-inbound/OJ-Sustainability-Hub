Kangaroo Island

What every client should know before they travel — and what your team should brief them on.

How to use
Brief clients on the four essentials before departure. The full conduct list below is your complete reference — share the relevant points based on each client's itinerary.
Brief your client on these four
01
Stay with the ranger at Seal Bay — never approach a sea lion
Seal Bay is the world's only fully accessible wild sea lion beach. The animals are wild, large and fast. Clients must keep their distance and never block a sea lion's path to the water.
02
Don't drive at dawn, dusk or night
Kangaroo Island has one of Australia's highest wildlife collision rates. Kangaroos, wallabies and echidnas are active in low light. Many hire agreements prohibit night driving outright.
03
Respect biosecurity — the island is fox and rabbit free
These species never established here, which is why the island's wildlife is so intact. Clients must not bring unchecked fruit, plants or soil, and should clean boots and gear before arrival.
04
Keep your distance from all wildlife — don't feed anything
From kangaroos to Glossy Black-Cockatoos, feeding or disturbing wildlife harms the animals. Nesting cockatoo areas at Baudin and Lathami are off-limits during breeding season, April to October.
Safety-critical — communicate every time
Road safety. The dawn/dusk/night driving rule isn't guidance — it's the single biggest risk on the island. Brief every self-driving client before departure, and confirm their hire agreement's night-driving terms.
Full conduct reference
Encourage
  • Acknowledge Ngarrindjeri Country on arrival
  • Stay with the ranger at Seal Bay
  • Keep 300m from whales, well clear of sea lions
  • Stay on marked tracks in national parks
  • Buy from local KI producers and operators
  • Use reef-safe sunscreen at beaches
  • Check park open and close times each day
  • Book the KI Indigenous Day Tour where it fits
  • Carry water and sun protection always
  • Report injured wildlife to KI Wildlife Rescue
Advise against
  • Feeding any wildlife, including kangaroos
  • Approaching, touching or blocking sea lions or seals
  • Driving at dawn, dusk or night
  • Removing plants, rocks or natural material
  • Littering anywhere, beaches and parks included
  • Entering marine sanctuary zones
  • Disturbing nesting cockatoos, April to October
  • Bringing unchecked food or plant material
  • Approaching Southern Right Whales within 300m
  • Buying souvenirs made from protected species
Ngarrindjeri cultural respect
Remarkable Rocks, Admirals Arch and Vivonne Bay hold deep spiritual significance for the Ngarrindjeri people. Encourage clients to engage with these sites respectfully, and to seek out the KI Indigenous Day Tour to understand the island through Ngarrindjeri eyes.
For park information and trail bookings: parks.sa.gov.au. To report injured wildlife: KI Wildlife Rescue, (08) 8553 9272. Reviewed at least every 12 months.
