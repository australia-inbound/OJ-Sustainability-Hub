Visitor Code of Conduct · Kangaroo Island

Nurrungaui — Kangaroo Island

Ngarrindjeri Country, South Australia. Please read and follow these guidelines before and during your visit to help protect this extraordinary island and support its ongoing recovery.

🦁 Wildlife — critical rules
Kangaroo Island's wildlife is wild and often at close range. Keep a respectful distance from all animals. At Seal Bay, always remain with a ranger and never approach or block sea lions — they can move quickly. Do not touch, feed or disturb any wildlife anywhere on the island.
✓ Do
  • ✓ Acknowledge Ngarrindjeri Country on arrival
  • ✓ Remain with a ranger at Seal Bay at all times
  • ✓ Keep 300m from whales, 5m from sea lions
  • ✓ Stay on marked tracks in national parks
  • ✓ Purchase from local KI producers and operators
  • ✓ Use reef-safe sunscreen at beaches
  • ✓ Check park open/close times before each day
  • ✓ Book the KI Indigenous Day Tour if available
  • ✓ Carry water and sun protection at all times
  • ✓ Report injured wildlife to KI Wildlife Rescue
✗ Do not
  • ✗ Feed any wildlife — including kangaroos
  • ✗ Approach, touch or block sea lions or seals
  • ✗ Drive at dawn, dusk or night — high wildlife collision risk
  • ✗ Remove any plants, rocks or natural material
  • ✗ Litter anywhere — including beaches and parks
  • ✗ Enter marine sanctuary zones (no-take, no-entry)
  • ✗ Approach nesting Glossy Black-Cockatoos (Apr–Oct)
  • ✗ Bring unchecked food or plant material to the island
  • ✗ Approach Southern Right Whales within 300m
  • ✗ Buy souvenirs made from protected species
🚗 Road safety
KI has one of Australia's highest wildlife collision rates. Never drive at dawn, dusk or night. Reduce speed near bushland at all times. Many hire car agreements prohibit night driving.
🌿 Ngarrindjeri cultural respect
Remarkable Rocks, Admirals Arch and Vivonne Bay hold deep spiritual significance. Engage with these sites respectfully. Seek out the KI Indigenous Day Tour to understand the island through Ngarrindjeri eyes.
⚠ Biosecurity — important
Kangaroo Island is free of foxes and rabbits — two of Australia's most destructive introduced species. Do not bring unchecked fruit, vegetables, plants or soil to the island. Clean boots and outdoor gear before arrival. Biosecurity protects the island's extraordinary natural heritage.

For park information and trail bookings visit parks.sa.gov.au · To report injured wildlife contact KI Wildlife Rescue: (08) 8553 9272
