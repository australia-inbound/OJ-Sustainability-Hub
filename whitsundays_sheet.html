Destination overview

Location & size
74 islands in the heart of the Great Barrier Reef Marine Park, off the central Queensland coast. Airlie Beach is the mainland gateway; the islands range from uninhabited national park to resort islands like Hamilton. Home to Whitehaven Beach — 7km of 98% pure silica sand.
Character
One of Australia's most beautiful island groups — sailing, snorkelling and beaches at the reef's edge. Most islands are protected national park. The region combines accessible reef experiences with the iconic Whitehaven Beach, making it a high-volume destination for Orange Journeys (#5 by pax).
Access
Fly to Whitsunday Coast Airport (Proserpine, PPP) or Hamilton Island Airport (HTI). Airlie Beach is the mainland hub for most cruises and transfers. Cruise Whitsundays and other operators run daily reef and island departures.
Best season
April–October (dry season) — best weather, calm seas, ideal sailing. November–May — warmer, stinger season (wear suits), occasional cyclones. The Great Barrier Reef Festival runs in Airlie Beach in early August.

02

Traditional Owners & sea Country

Traditional Owners
The Ngaro, Gia and Juru people are the Traditional Owners of the Whitsunday lands and seas. The Ngaro — a saltwater people — navigated these islands by canoe for thousands of years; their sites, including rock art and quarries, remain across the islands.
Cultural significance
The Ngaro Sea Trail links cultural sites across Hook, Whitsunday and South Molle islands. Operators increasingly acknowledge and interpret Ngaro connection to sea Country as part of responsible visitation.
Priority recommendation
Choose operators that acknowledge and interpret Ngaro sea Country. Encourage clients to walk part of the Ngaro Sea Trail or visit Nara Inlet's ancient rock art on Hook Island — among the oldest evidence of human occupation on the islands.

Sustainability & current status

Reef pressure
The Whitsundays sit within the Great Barrier Reef Marine Park and face the same climate pressures — the reef's fifth and sixth mass bleaching events (2024–25) affected reefs across the region. Responsible operator choice directly supports reef resilience.
Cyclone recovery
The region was severely hit by Cyclone Debbie in 2017; reefs and tourism infrastructure have largely recovered, demonstrating the area's resilience. Ongoing monitoring continues across the island reefs.
Positive programs
Eco Barge Clean Seas — marine debris removalReef 2050 PlanCrown-of-thorns controlEcotourism Australia certified operatorsEye on the Reef citizen science
Before you sell
Prioritise Ecotourism Australia Advanced Eco Certified operators — Cruise Whitsundays holds this for several products. A portion of reef fees funds Marine Park management. Be honest with clients about reef health while emphasising the conservation economy.
04

Biodiversity & environment

Why it matters
The Whitsundays' fringing reefs, seagrass meadows and island ecosystems support a rich web of marine and bird life — and Whitehaven's silica sand is a fragile, internationally rare feature.
Key wildlife
Green & loggerhead turtlesHumpback whales (Jul–Sep)DugongsManta raysReef fish & coralsWhitsunday hairy-nosed wombat (mainland)Sea eagles
Species to know
Green Sea Turtle
Endangered — nests on island beaches. Keep distance; never disturb nesting turtles or tracks.
Humpback Whale
Protected — migration Jul–Sep. The Whitsundays are a key calving area. Keep 100m (300m with calves).
Dugong
Vulnerable — seagrass grazer. Reduce vessel speed in seagrass areas.
Whitehaven Beach care
Whitehaven's 98% pure silica sand is internationally rare and fragile. Take nothing, leave nothing. Smoking and glass are banned. Use the designated facilities — there are no bins, so all rubbish must leave with visitors.
05

Getting there sustainably

By air
Whitsunday Coast Airport (Proserpine) and Hamilton Island Airport serve the region. Encourage direct routes and carbon offsetting for flights. Proserpine is the lower-cost mainland gateway; Hamilton Island flies direct for island-resort clients.
On the water
Cruise Whitsundays and other operators consolidate visitors onto larger, more efficient vessels — preferable to multiple small boats. Sailing tours (Camira, others) are inherently low-emission and a signature Whitsundays experience.
Mainland hub
Airlie Beach is compact and walkable; the lagoon, marina and town are easily covered on foot. No private car is needed for most itineraries once at Airlie Beach.
Choosing operators
Prioritise Ecotourism Australia Advanced Eco Certified operators and those partnering with Eco Barge Clean Seas. Sailing experiences offer the lowest-emission way to see the islands.
06

Accessibility

Overall
Moderate — improving, with some accessible vessels and resort facilities, though many island and beach experiences involve transfers over sand or steps.
Ferries & cruises
Cruise Whitsundays vessels offer accessible boarding at the Port of Airlie terminal — confirm requirements in advance. Some pontoon and reef experiences are wheelchair accessible; beach landings at Whitehaven are not.
Hamilton Island
The most accessible island base — buggies, accessible accommodation and level resort areas. A practical choice for clients with mobility needs who still want an island experience.
Whitehaven Beach
Access is by boat with a wet or soft-sand landing — not wheelchair accessible. Lookout experiences (Hill Inlet) involve a boardwalk and steps. Advise clients of this honestly when booking.
Plan ahead
Confirm vessel and transfer accessibility with each operator before booking. Hamilton Island is the strongest base for clients with mobility needs.
