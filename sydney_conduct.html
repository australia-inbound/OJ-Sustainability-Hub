Visitor Code of Conduct · Staff Briefing

Sydney

What every client should know before visiting Sydney — and what your team should brief them on.

How to use
Brief clients on the four essentials before departure. The full conduct reference below supports thorough briefings and responsible operator selection.
Brief your client on these four
01
Buy Aboriginal art only from verified outlets
Tourist precincts sell mass-produced fakes that harm Aboriginal artists. Direct clients to Blak Markets, Boomalli, Carriageworks or the Art Gallery of NSW Indigenous collection.
02
Travel car-free — use the Opal network
Sydney's trains, ferries, light rail and cycleways make a low-carbon visit easy. The ferry to Manly is part of the experience. Promote this as a genuine sustainability strength.
03
Swim between the flags
Rip currents at ocean beaches can be life-threatening. Clients must swim only between the red and yellow flags, and if caught in a rip, float and signal rather than swim against it.
04
Choose local and Aboriginal-owned businesses
Steer clients away from generic tourist-precinct options toward independent restaurants, local markets and Aboriginal-led experiences that keep value in the community.
Safety-critical — communicate every time
Beach & water safety. Always brief clients to swim between the red and yellow flags at ocean beaches, never swim outside flagged areas, and understand rip currents — float and signal for help rather than swimming against the current.
Full conduct reference
Encourage
  • Acknowledge Gadigal Country on arrival
  • Use the Opal card across all transport
  • Take the ferry to Manly
  • Wear a helmet when cycling (it's the law)
  • Eat and shop at local, independent businesses
  • Use reef-safe sunscreen at beaches
  • Attend an Aboriginal-led experience
  • Shop at Blak Markets for authentic art
Advise against
  • Hire a car for city sightseeing
  • Cycle without a helmet (fines apply)
  • Buy Aboriginal art without verifying authenticity
  • Swim outside flagged areas
  • Leave litter on beaches or harbour areas
  • Feed harbour wildlife
  • Book inauthentic Aboriginal experiences
  • Book large coach tours for harbour sightseeing
Gadigal Country & Aboriginal Sydney
Sydney is built on the Country of the Gadigal people of the Eora Nation. Encourage clients to experience authentic Aboriginal-led tours — the Royal Botanic Garden Aboriginal Heritage Tour, Dreamtime Southern X, and the Tribal Warrior harbour cruise.
For transport and accessibility: transportnsw.info. For beach safety: beachsafe.org.au. Reviewed at least every 12 months.
