Visitor Code of Conduct · Staff Briefing

Melbourne

What every client should know before visiting Melbourne — and what your team should brief them on.

How to use
Brief clients on the four essentials before departure. The full conduct reference below supports thorough briefings and responsible day-trip planning.
Brief your client on these four
01
No flash or phones at the Penguin Parade
At Phillip Island, artificial light disorients the Little Penguins. Photography and phone use are banned during the Parade — brief clients firmly, as this protects one of Victoria's most important wildlife colonies.
02
Travel car-free in the city
Melbourne's trams, trains and walkable centre make a car-free visit easy. The free City Circle tram and CBD tram zone cover the core. Promote this as the recommended approach.
03
Choose smaller-group day-trip operators
The Great Ocean Road and Phillip Island draw heavy coach traffic. Steer clients toward smaller-group or certified operators, and overnight stays that spread visitor load to regional towns.
04
Acknowledge Kulin Nation Country
Melbourne (Naarm) sits on Wurundjeri and Bunurong land. Encourage clients to include an Aboriginal-guided experience — often overlooked in a city known for coffee and laneways.
Safety-critical — communicate every time
Coastal safety on the Great Ocean Road. Brief clients that the coastline is geologically active and eroding — stay behind barriers at the Twelve Apostles and lookouts, never climb on cliff edges, and observe all beach safety signage.
Full conduct reference
Encourage
  • Acknowledge Wurundjeri and Bunurong Country
  • Use trams, trains and the Myki card
  • Walk and cycle the flat city centre
  • Include an Aboriginal-guided experience
  • Choose smaller-group day-trip operators
  • Consider overnight regional stays
  • Shop at Queen Victoria Market and local traders
  • Stay behind barriers at coastal lookouts
Advise against
  • Use flash or phones at the Penguin Parade
  • Hire a car for city sightseeing
  • Default to large coach day-trips
  • Climb cliff edges on the Great Ocean Road
  • Feed or disturb penguins, koalas or seals
  • Leave marked paths at coastal sites
  • Buy inauthentic Aboriginal souvenirs
  • Disturb nesting seabirds (hooded plovers)
Kulin Nation Country
Melbourne (Naarm) sits on the lands of the Kulin Nation — the Wurundjeri Woi-wurrung and Bunurong/Boon Wurrung peoples. Encourage clients to experience the Royal Botanic Gardens Aboriginal Heritage Walk or Bunjilaka Aboriginal Cultural Centre.
For transport: ptv.vic.gov.au. For Phillip Island: penguins.org.au. Reviewed at least every 12 months.
