Destination overview
Location & size
112 km long, 57 km wide — Australia's third largest island, located 13 km off the tip of the Fleurieu Peninsula in South Australia. Approximately 4,405 km² in area, with a resident population of around 4,700. One-third of the island is protected in national parks and conservation reserves.
Character
Kangaroo Island is one of Australia's premier nature-based tourism destinations — renowned for extraordinary wildlife diversity, pristine beaches, artisan food and wine producers, and a powerful post-bushfire resilience story. The island separated from mainland Australia around 10,000 years ago, producing high levels of endemic and sub-species wildlife found nowhere else on Earth.
Access
SeaLink ferry from Cape Jervis (45 mins) — the primary and preferred access option. Cape Jervis is 90 minutes south of Adelaide CBD. SeaLink's two new ferries Wanggami (kangaroo) and Ruwi (land) — named in Ngarrindjeri language — began service in 2025. Flights also available: Regional Express (Rex) from Adelaide to Kingscote Airport (KGC), multiple daily services.
Best season
Year-round destination. Spring (Sep–Nov) — wildflowers, whale calves, sea lion pups. Summer (Dec–Feb) — peak season, warm seas, long days. Autumn (Mar–May) — quieter, excellent wildlife spotting. Winter (Jun–Aug) — Southern Right Whales, fewer tourists, ideal for birdwatching.
Governing bodies
National Parks and Wildlife Service SA (NPWSSA) · Kangaroo Island Landscape Board · Kangaroo Island Council · Parks SA (Flinders Chase NP, Seal Bay Conservation Park, Kelly Hill Conservation Park)
Key legal frameworks
National Parks and Wildlife Act 1972 (SA) · Environment Protection and Biodiversity Conservation Act 1999 · Landscape South Australia Act 2019 · Kangaroo Island Wilderness Trail regulations

Ngarrindjeri Traditional Owners & Nurrungaui
Traditional Owners
Kangaroo Island is known to the Ngarrindjeri people as Nurrungaui — "the place of Nurrunderi," their revered creator. The Ngarrindjeri have deep cultural, spiritual and ancestral connections to the island and its surrounding waters. Their knowledge of the land, sea and sky informs modern conservation practice on the island.
Key cultural sites
Remarkable Rocks — connected to creation stories of Nurrunderi. Admirals Arch — significant in Ngarrindjeri spiritual narrative. Seal Bay — Pungari (the seal) is an ancient being in Ngarrindjeri story. Vivonne Bay — site of the Nurrunderi story of entering the Kalowinyeri (spirit world).
Cultural recognition
SeaLink's new ferries Wanggami and Ruwi were named through a community competition in close consultation with the Ngarrindjeri Aboriginal Council — drawing over 1,500 entries and requiring formal NAC approval.
Priority recommendation: The Kangaroo Island Indigenous Day Tour (SeaLink + Kool Tours), led by Ngarrindjeri Elder Mark Koolmatrie — 2024 SA Tourism Hall of Fame inductee — weaves Ngarrindjeri cultural history with the bushfire recovery story. Orange Journeys should actively include this in all KI itineraries.

Sustainability challenges & current status
Black Summer bushfires — 2019–20: Approximately 48% of the island burned — more than 210,000 hectares. Over 40,000 animals were killed. Two human lives were lost. Multiple tourism businesses including the iconic Southern Ocean Lodge were destroyed.
Recovery — 5 years on
Southern Ocean Lodge reopened in 2024. The $19.5M Flinders Chase Visitor Centre opened in 2024. The Kangaroo Island Wilderness Trail and Kelly Hill Conservation Park have both reopened. Tourism reached a bumper year in 2023 and continued strongly through 2024.
Current threats
1. Feral cats — primary ongoing threat to endemic wildlife. 39 cats removed from just outside the Western River Refuge fence line in 2024 alone.
2. Invasive weeds — spreading rapidly through post-fire landscapes.
3. Climate change — increasing fire frequency, drought conditions extending.
4. Visitor pressure — concentration at key sites requires careful management.
Positive programs
Western River Refuge (AWC) — feral predator-free fenced sanctuary · KI Landscape Board — 2,709 ha of native vegetation protected, 420 ha revegetated, 50,000 plants grown annually · Glossy Black-Cockatoo Recovery Program (since 1995) · DCCEEW $8.7M wildlife recovery investment
Orange Journeys monitoring note: Check Parks SA (parks.sa.gov.au) for any trail or park closures before departure. The Kangaroo Island Wilderness Trail requires advance booking year-round.

Biodiversity & natural environment
Why it matters
Kangaroo Island separated from mainland Australia approximately 10,000 years ago, producing extraordinary levels of endemism. Foxes and rabbits — which have devastated mainland ecosystems — have never established on the island, making it one of Australia's most intact ecological refuges.
Key wildlife
Australian Sea Lions (Seal Bay) · New Zealand Fur Seals (Admirals Arch) · Koalas · Kangaroos & Wallabies · Echidnas · Little Penguins · Southern Right Whales (Jun–Oct) · Leafy Sea Dragons · 250+ bird species
Glossy Black-Cockatoo (KI subspecies)
Endangered — 422 counted Sept 2024. Last refuge on Earth for this subspecies.
KI Dunnart
Critically endangered — endemic. Found east of Flinders Chase for first time in 40 years (2024).
Australian Sea Lion
Endangered — Seal Bay colony is the world's only fully accessible wild sea lion beach.
KI Southern Emu-wren
Endangered — endemic subspecies, severely impacted by 2019–20 fires.
Leafy Sea Dragon
Protected — best viewed at Emu Bay and Penneshaw.
Southern Right Whale
Protected — annual visitor Jun–Oct. Do not approach within 300m.
Wildlife interaction rules: At Seal Bay, visitors must remain with a ranger and keep a minimum distance from sea lions — do not approach, touch or block their path to the ocean. Glossy Black-Cockatoo breeding season is April–October — do not disturb nesting areas at Baudin and Lathami Conservation Parks.

Getting there sustainably
Ferry — recommended
SeaLink ferry from Cape Jervis to Penneshaw (45 mins) — the most sustainable and scenic access option. Cape Jervis is approximately 90 minutes south of Adelaide. The new vessels Wanggami and Ruwi (2025) are larger and more fuel-efficient than previous ferries.
By air
Regional Express (Rex) operates multiple daily services from Adelaide to Kingscote Airport (KGC) — approximately 30 minutes. Useful for tight itineraries or clients with limited mobility. However the ferry is a more sustainable and experientially richer option where time permits.
On the island
A hire car or guided tour is essential — there is no public transport network. Guided tours (Exceptional KI, Kangaroo Island Odysseys, SeaLink tours) are the most sustainable option as they consolidate passenger movements and use local, knowledgeable operators.
Road safety — critical: Kangaroo Island has one of the highest wildlife collision rates in Australia. Strictly avoid driving at dawn, dusk and night — this is when kangaroos, wallabies and echidnas are most active on roads. Many rental agreements prohibit night driving. Brief all clients on this before departure.

Accessibility
Overall rating
Moderate — improving. Key wildlife experiences are accessible with advance planning; remote wilderness areas are not. The new Flinders Chase Visitor Centre (2024) was built to contemporary accessibility standards.
Seal Bay
Boardwalk tours are wheelchair accessible — a guided boardwalk descends to the beach. The beach walk involves soft sand and is not accessible for wheelchairs. Advise clients to book the boardwalk tour and notify Parks SA of accessibility requirements in advance.
Remarkable Rocks
Short accessible path from the car park to the main viewing area. The rocks themselves involve uneven granite surfaces — not suitable for wheelchairs. The surrounding clifftop lookout is accessible and provides spectacular views.
Flinders Chase VC
New visitor centre (2024) is fully accessible with level entry, accessible toilets and immersive displays. Recommended as the starting point for all visitors with limited mobility.
Ferry access
SeaLink ferries are accessible — contact SeaLink directly when booking to arrange wheelchair boarding assistance and accessible seating. The new vessels (2025) have improved accessibility facilities.
Plan ahead: Kangaroo Island's remote character means accessible facilities outside main visitor sites are limited. Confirm specific accessibility requirements with each operator and Parks SA before booking any client with mobility needs.
