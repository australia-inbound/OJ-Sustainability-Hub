01

Destination overview

Location & size
112 km long, 57 km wide — Australia's third largest island, 13 km off the Fleurieu Peninsula. Around 4,405 km², resident population ~4,700. One-third is protected in national parks and conservation reserves.
Character
One of Australia's premier nature-based destinations — extraordinary wildlife, pristine beaches, artisan food and wine, and a powerful post-bushfire recovery story. The island separated from the mainland ~10,000 years ago, producing endemic wildlife found nowhere else on Earth.
Access
SeaLink ferry from Cape Jervis (45 min) is the primary, preferred route — Cape Jervis is 90 min south of Adelaide. New ferries Wanggami and Ruwi, named in Ngarrindjeri, entered service in 2025. Regional Express (Rex) also flies Adelaide to Kingscote (KGC) daily.
Best season
Spring — wildflowers, whale calves, sea lion pups. Summer — peak, warm seas. Autumn — quiet, excellent wildlife. Winter — Southern Right Whales, few tourists, prime birdwatching.

02

Ngarrindjeri Traditional Owners

Traditional Owners
Kangaroo Island is Nurrungaui to the Ngarrindjeri people — "the place of Nurrunderi," their revered creator. Their cultural, spiritual and ancestral connection to the island and its waters runs deep, and their knowledge of land, sea and sky informs conservation practice today.
Key cultural sites
Remarkable Rocks — creation stories of Nurrunderi. Admirals Arch — spiritual significance. Seal Bay — Pungari, the seal, an ancient being. Vivonne Bay — the story of entering the Kalowinyeri, the spirit world.
Cultural recognition
SeaLink's ferries Wanggami and Ruwi were named with the Ngarrindjeri Aboriginal Council through a community competition — 1,500+ entries, formal NAC approval.
Priority recommendation
Include the Kangaroo Island Indigenous Day Tour (SeaLink + Kool Tours) in all KI itineraries. Led by Ngarrindjeri Elder Mark Koolmatrie — 2024 SA Tourism Hall of Fame inductee — it weaves cultural history with the bushfire recovery story.
03

Sustainability & current status

Black Summer bushfires · 2019–20
~48% of the island burned — over 210,000 hectares. More than 40,000 animals killed, two lives lost, and major tourism businesses including Southern Ocean Lodge destroyed.
Recovery — five years on
Southern Ocean Lodge reopened in 2024. The $19.5M Flinders Chase Visitor Centre opened the same year. The Wilderness Trail and Kelly Hill Conservation Park have reopened. Tourism hit a record year in 2023 and held strong through 2024.
Current threats
Feral cats — the primary threat to endemic wildlife; 39 removed outside the Western River Refuge fence line in 2024 alone. Invasive weeds spreading through post-fire ground. Climate change lengthening fire seasons. Visitor pressure at key sites.
Positive programs

Before you sell
Check Parks SA (parks.sa.gov.au) for trail or park closures before departure. The Wilderness Trail requires advance booking year-round.

Biodiversity & environment

Why it matters
Separated from the mainland ~10,000 years ago, the island carries extraordinary endemism. Foxes and rabbits never established here, making it one of Australia's most intact ecological refuges.
Key wildlife
Australian Sea LionsNZ Fur SealsKoalasEchidnasLittle PenguinsSouthern Right WhalesLeafy Sea Dragons250+ birds
Species to know
Glossy Black-Cockatoo
Endangered — 422 counted Sept 2024. The last refuge on Earth for this subspecies.
KI Dunnart
Critically endangered · endemic — found east of Flinders Chase for the first time in 40 years (2024).
Australian Sea Lion
Endangered — Seal Bay is the world's only fully accessible wild sea lion beach.
KI Southern Emu-wren
Endangered · endemic — severely hit by the 2019–20 fires.
Southern Right Whale
Protected — winter visitor, Jun–Oct. Keep 300 m clear.
Wildlife rules
At Seal Bay, stay with the ranger and never approach, touch or block a sea lion's path to the water. Don't disturb Glossy Black-Cockatoo nesting areas at Baudin and Lathami during breeding season, April–October.
05

Getting there sustainably

Ferry — recommended
SeaLink from Cape Jervis to Penneshaw (45 min) is the most sustainable and scenic route. The 2025 vessels Wanggami and Ruwi are larger and more fuel-efficient than the boats they replaced.
By air
Rex flies Adelaide to Kingscote (~30 min) — useful for tight itineraries or limited mobility, though the ferry is the richer, lower-carbon choice where time allows.
On the island
A hire car or guided tour is essential — there's no public transport. Guided tours (Exceptional KI, Kangaroo Island Odysseys, SeaLink) are the most sustainable option, consolidating movements and using local guides.
Road safety — critical
Kangaroo Island has one of Australia's highest wildlife collision rates. Never drive at dawn, dusk or night, when kangaroos, wallabies and echidnas are active. Many hire agreements prohibit night driving — brief every client before departure.
06

Accessibility

Overall
Moderate and improving. Key wildlife experiences are accessible with planning; remote wilderness areas are not. The 2024 Flinders Chase Visitor Centre meets contemporary accessibility standards.
Seal Bay
Boardwalk tours are wheelchair accessible. The beach-level walk crosses soft sand and is not. Book the boardwalk tour and notify Parks SA of requirements in advance.
Remarkable Rocks
Accessible path from the car park to the viewing area; the granite itself is uneven and not wheelchair-suitable. The clifftop lookout is accessible with spectacular views.
Ferry
SeaLink ferries are accessible — arrange boarding assistance and accessible seating when booking. The 2025 vessels improved on previous facilities.
Plan ahead
Facilities outside the main visitor sites are limited. Confirm specific requirements with each operator and Parks SA before booking any client with mobility needs.
