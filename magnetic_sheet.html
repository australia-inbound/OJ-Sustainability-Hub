Destination overview

Location & size
Magnetic Island (Yunbenun) lies 8km off Townsville in north Queensland — a short ferry ride within the Great Barrier Reef World Heritage Area. The island covers 5,184 ha, of which 78% is protected national and conservation park. Townsville is the mainland hub and a major marine-science centre.
Character
A relaxed, nature-rich island known for Northern Australia's largest wild koala colony, 23 bays and beaches, and 26km of walking trails. Much quieter and more affordable than the southern reef hubs, with a strong local conservation community.
Access
Fly to Townsville Airport (TSV), then a short SeaLink ferry (about 20–25 min) from Townsville to Nelly Bay on Magnetic Island. Ferries run frequently throughout the day and are wheelchair accessible.
Best season
April–November (dry season) — warm, low humidity, ideal for walking and wildlife. December–March — hot, humid, wet season, stinger season in the water. Koalas are visible year-round along the Forts Walk.

02

Wulgurukaba Traditional Owners & Yunbenun

Traditional Owners
The Wulgurukaba people — the 'canoe people' — are the Traditional Custodians of Magnetic Island, known to them as Yunbenun. They have an enduring connection to the island and nearby mainland stretching back thousands of years, evidenced by shell middens, stone tools and art sites.
Cultural significance
Yunbenun means 'spirit of the cassowary' in the Wulgurukaba language. The Wulgurukaba work with Queensland Parks & Wildlife to co-manage the island. In 2024 the Queensland Government proposed renaming the national park Yunbenun National Park in recognition of Wulgurukaba custodianship.
Priority recommendation
Acknowledge Wulgurukaba connection to Yunbenun in client communications. Use the traditional name alongside Magnetic Island, and choose experiences that interpret the island's deep Aboriginal history, not only its WWII and natural heritage.

Sustainability & current status

Protected island
78% of Magnetic Island is protected national and conservation park, and its waters fall within the Great Barrier Reef Marine Park. This makes responsible visitation relatively straightforward, but the koala colony and fringing reefs require care.
Community conservation
Magnetic Island has an unusually active local conservation community for its size (around 2,400 residents), supported by Townsville's status as a marine-science hub. Groups work on koala monitoring, reef health and marine debris.
Positive programs
Magnetic Island koala monitoringGreat Barrier Reef Foundation community projectsReef HQ Aquarium (Townsville)Marine debris removalCitizen science
Before you sell
Emphasise Magnetic Island's exceptional protected-area status and its wild koala colony — a genuine drawcard. Brief clients on koala-viewing etiquette (observe quietly, never disturb) and the island's car-light character.

Biodiversity & environment

Why it matters
Magnetic Island is one of the most ecologically diverse islands of the Great Barrier Reef — combining woodland, granite headlands, fringing reef, seagrass and mangrove habitats in a small, accessible area.
Key wildlife
Wild koalas (largest colony in N. Australia)Allied rock-wallabiesGreen turtlesDugongsBush stone-curlewsPossumsFringing-reef fish & coral
Species to know
Koala
Protected — Northern Australia's largest wild colony. Best seen on the Forts Walk. Observe quietly; never disturb.
Allied Rock-wallaby
Protected — lives among the granite boulders. Do not feed; feeding harms their health and behaviour.
Green Sea Turtle
Endangered — rests on fringing reefs. Keep distance when snorkelling.
Dugong
Vulnerable — grazes the seagrass meadows. A key species for the island's waters.
Wildlife etiquette
Do not feed any wildlife, including rock-wallabies (despite popular feeding spots). Observe koalas quietly from the track and never disturb them. The fringing reefs are sensitive — use reef-safe sunscreen and don't touch coral.
05

Getting there sustainably

By air & ferry
Fly to Townsville (TSV), then the SeaLink ferry to Magnetic Island (20–25 min). The ferry is the only access and is itself a low-impact, scenic crossing within the Marine Park.
On the island
Magnetic Island is best explored without a private car — local buses connect the bays, and walking trails link key sites. Some visitors hire e-bikes or the iconic open-top 'Barbie cars', though buses are the lowest-impact option.
Lower-carbon appeal
The compact island, frequent ferries and bus network make a genuinely car-free visit easy — a strong sustainability point for clients, and more affordable than the southern reef hubs.
Sustainable framing
Magnetic Island is one of the easiest reef destinations to visit car-free. Promote the ferry-plus-bus combination and walking trails as both the lowest-impact and most authentic way to experience Yunbenun.
06

Accessibility

Overall
Moderate — the ferry and main bays are accessible, but the island's signature wildlife walks involve uneven terrain.
Ferry
SeaLink ferries are designed to accommodate wheelchair users — confirm requirements when booking. Nelly Bay terminal is accessible.
Around the island
The local bus service connects the main bays and is the most accessible way to get around. Horseshoe Bay and Nelly Bay have accessible foreshore areas; some beaches have better access than others.
Forts Walk & koalas
The Forts Walk — the best koala-viewing trail — is an unsealed uphill track and is not wheelchair accessible. Bounce Magnetic Island's koala and wildlife park offers a more accessible alternative for close wildlife encounters.
Plan ahead
For clients with mobility needs, the koala and wildlife park is a more accessible alternative to the Forts Walk. Confirm ferry and accommodation accessibility in advance.
