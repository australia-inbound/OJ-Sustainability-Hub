Visitor Guide · Travelling Responsibly

Great Barrier Reef

We can't wait to welcome you to the Great Barrier Reef. A few simple things will help you explore it safely, and help protect this living wonder for generations to come.

Four things to know before you go
01
Use reef-safe sunscreen — never touch the coral
Standard sunscreens damage coral at very low concentrations; TGA-approved reef-safe formulas are essential. Touching, standing on or kicking coral kills it. Clients must float, not stand.
02
Book only GBRMPA-permitted, High Standard operators
Reef visitation fees fund the Marine Park's management and monitoring. High Standard Tourism Operators meet the strongest environmental and interpretation benchmarks, always choose them.
03
Keep your distance from all marine wildlife
Stay 3–5m from free-swimming wildlife, keep 100m from whales (300m with calves), and never chase, touch, feed or block any animal. Feeding alters natural behaviour.
04
Take nothing — coral and shells are protected
It is illegal to take, buy or export coral, shells, turtle products or any CITES-listed species, whether sold commercially or not. If in doubt, don't purchase.
Please take extra care
In-water safety. Clients must only enter the water with permitted operators, wear stinger suits in season (Nov–May), use the buddy system, and attend every crew safety briefing. Stinger and current risks are real.
Exploring the Great Barrier Reef responsibly
Encourage
  • Use reef-safe, TGA-approved sunscreen
  • Wear a wetsuit or stinger suit in season
  • Stay 3–5m from all free-swimming wildlife
  • Use moorings, never anchor on coral
  • Attend every crew safety briefing
  • Choose GBRMPA High Standard operators
  • Report coral damage or pollution to GBRMPA
  • Join the Reef Census citizen science program
Advise against
  • Touch, stand on or kick coral
  • Chase, touch, feed or block marine wildlife
  • Take coral, shells or marine material
  • Discharge rubbish, fuel or plastics overboard
  • Approach whales within 100m (300m with calves)
  • Approach whale sharks within 3m
  • Enter green (preservation) zones
  • Buy souvenirs made from coral or reef species
Traditional Owner sea Country
More than 70 Traditional Owner groups hold connections to the reef, cared for over 60,000 years. We'd encourage you to choose Indigenous-guided reef experiences such as Dreamtime Dive & Snorkel, which weave saltwater culture with reef ecology.
For reef conditions and zoning: gbrmpa.gov.au. Report incidents to the Reef Authority. Reviewed at least every 12 months.
