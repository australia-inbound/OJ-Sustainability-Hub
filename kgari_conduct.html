K'gari

What every client should know before they travel — and what your team should brief them on.

How to use
Brief clients on the four essentials before departure. The full conduct list below is your complete reference — share the relevant points based on each client's itinerary.
Brief your client on these four
01
Never feed or approach wongari (dingoes)
Keep children within arm's reach at all times. This is the purest dingo population in eastern Australia, and food habituation is driving rising aggression.
02
Always say "K'gari," never "Fraser Island"
The World Heritage property itself was formally renamed in 2025, honouring Butchulla wishes. The old name commemorated a woman whose story led directly to Butchulla dispossession.
03
Time all beach driving to the tides
75 Mile Beach is a gazetted road, but driving outside safe tide windows risks vehicles and damages the ecosystem. 4WD and local knowledge are essential.
04
Check the conditions report before every departure
Track and beach closures change seasonally — a multi-year closure is currently in place near Sandy Cape. Never assume last trip's route is still open.
Safety-critical — communicate every time
Dingo safety. Never feed a wongari. If approached, never run — stand tall and back away slowly. Keep children within arm's reach at all times, especially during whelping season, 1 June–30 September.
Full conduct reference
Encourage
  • Acknowledge Butchulla Country
  • Say "K'gari," never "Fraser Island"
  • Use only licensed barge operators and 4WD vehicles
  • Check tide tables before any beach driving
  • Visit Wanggoolba Creek and Central Station respectfully
  • Keep children within arm's reach near wongari
  • Store all food and rubbish securely, every stop
  • Check the QPWS conditions report before departure
  • Recommend Kingfisher Bay Resort where it fits the brief
Advise against
  • Feeding or approaching wongari (dingoes)
  • Running if a dingo approaches
  • Calling the island "Fraser Island"
  • Driving on the beach outside safe tide windows
  • Swimming at unpatrolled or rip-affected beaches
  • Removing sand, rocks or vegetation
  • Leaving food or rubbish accessible in camps or vehicles
  • Entering closed track or beach sections
  • Disturbing nesting or migrating wildlife
Butchulla Country
Wanggoolba Creek at Central Station is sacred to the Butchulla people. Encourage Butchulla-guided or Butchulla-interpreted experiences where available, and always use the island's correct name.
For conditions reports: parks.qld.gov.au/kgari-fraser. To report wildlife incidents: QPWS. Reviewed at least every 12 months.
