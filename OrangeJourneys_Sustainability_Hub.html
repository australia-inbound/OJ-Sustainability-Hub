How to use these files

For the sales & operations team
1
Before selling a destination
Read the relevant destination file before speaking with clients. Pay particular attention to the visitor code of conduct, cultural considerations and any active environmental alerts (e.g. reef bleaching, bushfire closures).
2
At time of booking confirmation
Share the visitor code of conduct section with clients in their booking confirmation documentation. For Uluru, the photography rules and climbing ban must be communicated without exception.
3
When selecting operators
Use the preferred operators section as a starting point. Prioritise Indigenous-led and certified operators (HSTO for GBR, ECO-certified for Blue Mountains). Always verify certifications before contracting new suppliers.
4
Guide briefings
Tour leaders and local guides should be briefed from these files before each departure. The cultural protocols and dos & don'ts sections are specifically designed to be communicated verbally to clients at destination arrival.
5
Annual review
Each file should be reviewed and updated at minimum every 12 months, or immediately following any significant destination event (reef bleaching, park closure, new Indigenous tourism development). Update the "Last updated" date on each sheet.
