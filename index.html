Environmental pressures
& climate context

Understanding Australia's
critical challenges

Australia's most iconic tourism destinations are under increasing pressure from climate change. Understanding these challenges is essential for responsible tour operators — it shapes how we sell destinations, how we brief clients, and how we engage with local conservation efforts.

🔥
2019 — 2020

Black Summer bushfires

The 2019–20 Black Summer was the most catastrophic bushfire season ever recorded in Australia. Fuelled by record-breaking drought, extreme heat and high winds — all intensified by climate change — fires burned from July 2019 to March 2020, consuming around 24 million hectares across New South Wales, Victoria, Queensland and South Australia.

2024 — 2025

Great Barrier Reef mass bleaching

The Great Barrier Reef experienced its fifth mass bleaching event in 2024, followed immediately by a sixth in 2025 — only the second time in history the reef has faced back-to-back bleaching years. The 2024–25 AIMS report recorded the largest annual coral cover decline in 39 years of monitoring, driven almost entirely by climate change-induced ocean heat stress.

How to use these files

For the sales & operations team
1
Before selling a destination
Read the relevant destination file before speaking with clients. Pay particular attention to the visitor code of conduct, cultural considerations and any active environmental alerts such as reef bleaching or bushfire closures.
2
At time of booking confirmation
Share the visitor code of conduct section with clients in their booking confirmation documentation. For Uluru, the photography rules and climbing ban must be communicated without exception.
3
When selecting operators
Use the preferred operators section as a starting point. Prioritise Indigenous-led and certified operators. Always verify certifications before contracting new suppliers.
4
Guide briefings
Tour leaders and local guides should be briefed from these files before each departure. The cultural protocols and dos & don'ts sections are specifically designed to be communicated verbally to clients at destination arrival.
5
Annual review
Each file should be reviewed and updated at minimum every 12 months, or immediately following any significant destination event. Update the "Last updated" date on each sheet when changes are made.
