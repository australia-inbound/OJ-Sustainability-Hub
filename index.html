Climate context

Why responsible selling matters

Australia's most iconic destinations are under increasing pressure from climate change. Understanding these challenges is part of selling them responsibly — it shapes how we brief clients and how we engage with local conservation efforts.

2019–20Black Summer bushfires

The 2019–20 Black Summer was the most catastrophic bushfire season on record in Australia. Fuelled by record drought, extreme heat and high winds, fires burned from July 2019 to March 2020, consuming around 24 million hectares across New South Wales, Victoria, Queensland and South Australia.

2024–25Great Barrier Reef mass bleaching

The Great Barrier Reef experienced its fifth mass bleaching event in 2024, followed immediately by a sixth in 2025 — the second time in history the reef has faced back-to-back bleaching years. The 2024–25 AIMS report recorded the largest annual coral cover decline in 39 years of monitoring, driven by climate-change-induced ocean heat stress.

How to use these files

For the sales & operations team
1
Before selling a destination
Read the relevant destination file before speaking with clients. Pay particular attention to the visitor code of conduct, cultural considerations and any active environmental alerts such as reef bleaching or bushfire closures.
2
At time of booking confirmation
Share the visitor code of conduct with clients in their booking confirmation. For Uluru, the photography rules and climbing ban must be communicated without exception.
3
When selecting operators
Use the preferred operators guidance as a starting point. Prioritise Indigenous-led and certified operators, and always verify certifications before contracting new suppliers.
4
Guide briefings
Tour leaders and local guides should be briefed from these files before each departure. The essentials and conduct points are designed to be communicated verbally to clients at destination arrival.
5
Annual review
Each file is reviewed at minimum every 12 months, or immediately following any significant destination event. The "last updated" date on each sheet reflects the most recent review.
